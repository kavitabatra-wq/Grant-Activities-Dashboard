Methodology & notes

  • Award rate (count) — the share of proposals that were funded, calculated as grants awarded ÷ proposals submitted. It is computed per fiscal year in the trend chart, and as a pooled total (awards ÷ submissions) for the selected range in the summary card. e.g. FY26: 46 ÷ 70 = 66%.
  • Funding success (dollars) — the share of requested dollars that were awarded, calculated as $ awarded ÷ $ requested. Likewise computed per fiscal year in the trend chart, and pooled across the selected range in the summary card. e.g. FY24: $9.06M ÷ $10.74M = 84%.
  • Both rates use each fiscal year's own submissions and awards. Because a grant is often awarded in a later year than it was submitted, a single year's dollars or awards may trace back to earlier proposals — so annual rates show the funding climate of that year, not the exact yield of that year's applications.
  • Figures encompass total grant proposals and awards, including clinical trials.
  • The elevated FY22 activity — particularly the $196.0M requested — is largely attributable to ARPA proposals and awards received that year.
  • A prior revision noted that FY25 reflected only the first quarter (July–September); current figures are as of the last-updated date above.
